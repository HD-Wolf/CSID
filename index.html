Intro

As computer science students (situated in North America), we are taught to value particular definitions of “computer science” and “computer scientist.” Issues of ethics, social justice, and the environment are typically not part of these definitions. Rather, computer science is often presented as immaterial and abstract (mathematical). When the materiality of computer science is addressed (e.g. in software engineering), larger webs of labour and extraction remain hidden. The purpose of this website is to familiarize computer science students with concepts that they can use to think critically about computer science and their own experiences in computer science.


Questions to Consider

The following are some questions students might consider as they work:

  • Who is this for?
  • Is this something the users/target audience actually want? Are users involved in the design process? Have you gotten user feedback?
  • What processes of extraction are taking place to create it?
